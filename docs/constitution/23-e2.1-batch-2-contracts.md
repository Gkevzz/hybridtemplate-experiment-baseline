# E2.1 — Batch 2 Component Contracts

Authoritative contracts for the second wave of the E2.1 registry. Persisted under E2 authorization. Eight-field contract template per E2. Batch 1 lives at mem://editorial/e2.1-batch-1-contracts and is not modified by this file.

Scope: structural / page-architecture components governing the review and guide page skeletons.

---

## 1. ReviewSection

- **Purpose**: Define one of the fixed 17 review sections as a discrete, semantically meaningful region of a casino review page.
- **Editorial meaning**: Structural unit of the canonical 17-section review architecture. Carries Layer 1/2/3 discipline within its own boundary. The "om operatören" ReviewSection provides narrative context but never duplicates the OperatorIdentity table.
- **Reader problem solved**: Reader can navigate, scan, and trust a review because every review uses the same fixed sectional skeleton in the same order.
- **Usage conditions**: Operator review pages only. Sections render in the fixed canonical order defined in mem://editorial/review-framework; order is never altered. Sections are conditional in presence but never reordered. Section-level data gaps render DataStatusNotice at the top of the affected ReviewSection. Default maximum one InfoBlock per ReviewSection.
- **Required relationships**:
  - Belongs to the fixed 17-section architecture; not a free-form container.
  - Hosts SourceReference / VerificationStatus for factual claims within its body.
  - Hosts DataStatusNotice at the top when its own data is partial, pending, or stale.
  - Anchored by TableOfContents and (where applicable) by TrustProfile category links.
  - "om operatören" ReviewSection complements OperatorIdentity but never duplicates the identity table.
- **Supported states**: Standard (rendered with verified content); Partial (rendered with DataStatusNotice at top); Pending (rendered as scoped placeholder using "Verifiering tillkommer", never silently empty); Hidden (only when constitutionally optional and not applicable; never reordered).
- **Forbidden uses**: Never reordered. Never invented or renamed ad hoc. Never used outside review pages. Never a marketing surface, CTA region, or bonus-promo block. Never duplicates OperatorIdentity, TrustProfile, or EditorialVerdict content. Never stacked with more than one InfoBlock by default; additional InfoBlock requires a distinct reader-comprehension need.
- **Related components**: TableOfContents, OperatorIdentity, TrustProfile, EditorialVerdict, DataStatusNotice, VerificationStatus, SourceReference, InfoBlock, RiskNotice, ResponsibleGamblingBlock.

## 2. ArticleLead

- **Purpose**: Provide a calm, factual opening paragraph that frames what the page is and who it is for, in plain Swedish.
- **Editorial meaning**: Editorial entry surface. Sets context and scope, never sells. Layer 1/2 only; never Layer 3 recommendation.
- **Reader problem solved**: Reader immediately understands what they are reading and whether it is relevant to them, without hype.
- **Usage conditions**: Reviews and guides only in Batch 2. Renders once at the top of the page, before OperatorIdentity, TrustProfile, and EditorialVerdict in reviews. Comparison, listing, category, and homepage opener patterns are explicitly deferred to later contracts.
- **Required relationships**:
  - Precedes the OperatorIdentity → TrustProfile → EditorialVerdict cluster on reviews.
  - On guides, precedes the body and any TableOfContents that ultimately ships.
  - Never absorbs or replaces TrustProfile, EditorialVerdict, OperatorIdentity, or ResponsibleGamblingBlock content.
- **Supported states**: Standard (review or guide lead, full factual framing); Compact (short framing on shorter guides); Pending (placeholder framing using "Verifiering tillkommer" when key facts are unverified — never invented).
- **Forbidden uses**: No commercial CTA, bonus headline figure, urgency, FOMO, or "bäst/topp/vinnare" framing. Never substitutes for EditorialVerdict. Never used on comparison, listing, category, or homepage surfaces in Batch 2. Never marketing copy. Never invents facts.
- **Related components**: OperatorIdentity, TrustProfile, EditorialVerdict, TableOfContents, DataStatusNotice.

## 3. OperatorIdentity

- **Purpose**: Present the operator's verified Layer 1 identity facts (juridisk operatör, licens, säte, ägarstruktur, kontakt) in a stable, scannable surface.
- **Editorial meaning**: Pure Layer 1 identity surface. No analysis, no opinion, no recommendation. Anchors all downstream Layer 2/3 work on the page.
- **Reader problem solved**: Reader can confirm who they are dealing with before they read assessment or consider commercial action.
- **Usage conditions**: Operator review pages only. Renders in the upper review identity cluster after ArticleLead and before TrustProfile. Every field is either verified (with VerificationStatus + SourceReference) or rendered as "Verifiering tillkommer". Operator logos are not required in v1; if shown, they must be sourced/rights-safe, static, identity-only, and visually subordinate to factual identity fields. No animated, oversized, promotional, or hero-style logo treatment.
- **Required relationships**:
  - Each factual field pairs with VerificationStatus and SourceReference; sources resolve into SourcesRegister.
  - Precedes TrustProfile and EditorialVerdict on the page.
  - The "om operatören" ReviewSection complements but never duplicates this table.
  - DataStatusNotice renders adjacent when core identity fields are pending or stale.
- **Supported states**: Complete (all core identity fields verified); Partial (one or more fields pending — visible "Verifiering tillkommer", DataStatusNotice when material); Stale (verification past freshness threshold — DataStatusNotice required).
- **Forbidden uses**: Never a brand-promo header or marketing hero. No animated, oversized, or promotional logo treatment. No CTAs, bonuses, awards, or score chips. Never invents identity facts. Never duplicated as a second identity block elsewhere on the page. Never replaces TrustProfile or EditorialVerdict.
- **Related components**: ArticleLead, TrustProfile, EditorialVerdict, VerificationStatus, SourceReference, SourcesRegister, DataStatusNotice, ReviewSection ("om operatören").

## 4. InfoBlock

- **Purpose**: Surface a discrete, scannable cluster of supporting facts or definitions that materially aids reader comprehension of the surrounding content.
- **Editorial meaning**: Comprehension aid. Layer 1 or carefully scoped Layer 2 only. Never decoration, never rhythm filler.
- **Reader problem solved**: Reader needs a focused, at-a-glance reference (definitions, key parameters, scoped facts) without leaving the flow.
- **Usage conditions**: Default maximum one InfoBlock per ReviewSection. Additional uses within a single ReviewSection require a distinct reader-comprehension need that the surrounding prose cannot satisfy. In long-form guides, InfoBlock usage is governed by editorial review under the same anti-decorative discipline.
- **Required relationships**:
  - Lives inside a ReviewSection (on reviews) or inside a guide body region.
  - Facts inside an InfoBlock follow the same VerificationStatus / SourceReference rules as inline claims.
  - Never substitutes for TrustProfile, EditorialVerdict, OperatorIdentity, DataStatusNotice, RiskNotice, or ResponsibleGamblingBlock.
- **Supported states**: Standard (single comprehension-focused block); Pending (rendered with "Verifiering tillkommer" where data is unverified — never invented).
- **Forbidden uses**: Never used for rhythm filler, visual variety, marketing emphasis, or decorative callouts. Never a CTA surface. Never a substitute for required disclosures or trust components. Never stacked beyond the default maximum without distinct comprehension justification.
- **Related components**: ReviewSection, VerificationStatus, SourceReference, DataStatusNotice, RiskNotice.

---

## 5. TableOfContents

- **Purpose**: Provide stable, predictable in-page navigation for long-form content.
- **Editorial meaning**: Navigational scaffolding bound to a stable, semantic section model. Never a marketing or promo surface.
- **Reader problem solved**: Reader can orient, scan, and jump to the part of a long page they care about.
- **Usage conditions**: Reviews render TableOfContents against the fixed 17-section ReviewSection architecture. Guides render TableOfContents only when stable, semantic, editorially approved H2 slugs exist; without such a convention, guide TOCs are deferred and render in the Hidden state. Never built against ad-hoc or unstable H2 slugs.
- **Required relationships**:
  - On reviews, anchors map 1:1 to ReviewSection instances in canonical order.
  - On guides, anchors map to approved H2 slugs only.
  - Coexists with ArticleLead (which precedes it) and with the page body it indexes.
- **Supported states**: Standard (review; full canonical section list, conditional sections omitted from anchors when Hidden but order preserved); Guide-enabled (guides with approved slug convention); Hidden (guides without an approved slug convention, or pages too short to warrant a TOC).
- **Forbidden uses**: Never reorders ReviewSection order. Never invents section names. Never built against ad-hoc/unstable H2 slugs. Never a promotional surface, CTA region, or sponsored-link list. Never duplicates ArticleLead content.
- **Related components**: ReviewSection, ArticleLead, OperatorIdentity, TrustProfile, EditorialVerdict.
