# HybridBonus — E3: Editorial Implementation Roadmap

**Status:** Approved — Immutable except by amendment.

**Scope:** Constitutional roadmap only. No code, CSS, components, visual specs, copy, or new editorial principles.

**Canonical persistence target (on later approval):** `mem://editorial/e3-implementation-roadmap`

**Operational status register (separate, non-constitutional):** `mem://editorial/e3-status`

**Position in hierarchy:** Operating System → Editorial Constitution → E0 → E1 → E2 → **E3** → HybridBonus Visual Bible

---

## 1. Purpose

E3 is the constitutional roadmap that translates the Operating System, Editorial Constitution, E0, E1, and E2 into a staged implementation sequence.

E3 answers what gets built first, what gets migrated first, what waits, what must be neutralized before public sharing, what is safe for Build Mode, what requires Plan Mode, and how the current Lovable prototype shell is migrated without wholesale rework.

The current Lovable prototype is a non-canonical shell — a starting condition to be migrated. It is not approved editorial output and creates no precedent.

E3 never owns editorial philosophy, visual language, component definitions/contracts/families, engineering architecture, code, CSS, design tokens, copy, casino data truth, or SEO strategy beyond sequencing.

---

## 2. Relationship to Other Constitutions

E3 inherits all decisions from the Operating System, Editorial Constitution, E0, E1, and E2 and never restates or redefines them.

E3 sequences work; it does not legislate what the work means.

E3 may name components, families, principles, and visual states only by reference — never by redefinition.

Lower layers, including the Visual Bible and engineering execution, may not invent phases, gates, or migration rules outside E3. New phases, gates, or migration rules require an E3 amendment.

Silence is not permission for editorial implementation work, phase/gate changes, component migration, IA, and public-readiness decisions. Routine maintenance that does not affect editorial meaning, components, governance, IA, or public-readiness gates may proceed under the Work Mode Rules.

---

## 3. Implementation Philosophy

E3 is governed by five constitutional statements. They are not guidelines; they are binding on every implementation decision E3 authorizes.

1. **Migration over rebuild.** The prototype is a starting condition, not a baseline to preserve. Work moves prototype surfaces toward constitutional conformance; it does not rebuild the system from scratch and does not preserve prototype elements that violate E0/E1/E2.

2. **No public exposure of unconstitutional output.** Any surface that does not yet conform to E0/E1/E2 must be neutralized, gated, or removed from public exposure before it is reachable by a visitor. Public exposure of non-conforming editorial output is treated as a constitutional breach.

3. **Sequencing follows trust.** Phases are ordered so that trust-bearing surfaces (disclosure, responsible gambling, sourcing, Trust Profile) are constitutionally supported before commercial or decision-driving surfaces are activated.

4. **Smallest safe step.** Each unit of work is the smallest change that moves a surface from its current state to the next constitutionally valid state. Large reorganizations are decomposed; no single step both invents structure and exposes it publicly.

5. **Roadmap is a sequence, not a schedule.** E3 defines order, entry/exit criteria, and dependencies. It does not define dates, durations, velocity, or resource allocation. Scheduling is operational and lives outside the constitution.

## 4. Phase Model (8 phases, 0–7)

Each phase declares Goal / Entry / Exit / Not in this phase. Later phases may not begin until prior-phase exit criteria are met for the surfaces they touch.

### Phase 0 — Constitutional Alignment

- **Goal.** Confirm Operating System, Editorial Constitution, E0, E1, and E2 are loaded, current, and referenced.
- **Entry.** All upstream constitutional rules are available through their canonical memory documents, index references, or approved constitutional architecture.
- **Exit.** All upstream constitutions are read, understood, and referenced as binding for every downstream decision.
- **Not in this phase.** Surface changes, component migration, copy editing, or any public-facing modification.

### Phase 1 — Prototype public-risk neutralization

- **Goal.** Neutralize prototype elements whose public exposure would breach E0/E1/E2 (misleading ranking language, placeholder commercial CTAs adjacent to incomplete reviews, unverified trust claims, fake data presented as real). Neutralization only — removal and replacement belong to later phases where the relevant E2 components and page systems are available.
- **Entry.** Constitutional alignment is complete; current prototype surfaces are catalogued.
- **Exit.** Every public-risk prototype element is neutralized, removed from public reach, or explicitly gated; no unconstitutional output remains publicly reachable.
- **Not in this phase.** Component replacement, new page construction, content authoring, or reintroduction of neutralized elements.

### Phase 2 — Core Component Application

- **Goal.** Apply approved E2 components to surfaces already constitutionally supported.
- **Entry.** Phase 1 neutralization is complete for the target surfaces; E2 contracts are available.
- **Exit.** Target surfaces render using E2 components within their documented contracts; no unapproved components remain on those surfaces.
- **Not in this phase.** New component families, component-contract changes, trust-system wiring, or commercial activation.

### Phase 3 — Trust & Disclosure Systems

- **Goal.** Apply Trust Profile, disclosure, responsible-gambling, and source/verification surfaces per E0/E1/E2 before any commercial decision surfaces are activated.
- **Entry.** Core E2 components are applied to the target surfaces; trust-family components are available.
- **Exit.** Every surface that supports a decision point displays the required trust, disclosure, and responsible-gambling elements before any commercial action.
- **Not in this phase.** Commercial CTAs, outbound links, bonus claims, or public-readiness sign-off.

### Phase 4 — Review-Page System

- **Goal.** Implement the 17-section review architecture using E2 components.
- **Entry.** Trust and disclosure systems are in place for review pages.
- **Exit.** Every sentence on every review page conforms to the 3-layer fact/analysis/recommendation rule; Layer 3 appears only in slutsats; every Layer 1 factual claim has a source slot.
- **Not in this phase.** Comparison pages, guide pages, new page types, or SEO strategy changes.

### Phase 5 — Comparison & Guide Surfaces

- **Goal.** Build comparison and guide pages per editorial structures.
- **Entry.** Review-page system is stable; editorial structures for comparison and guide pages are available.
- **Exit.** Comparison and guide pages render within E2 components, use trade-off framing, follow player-profile structure, and contain no total scores or ranking language.
- **Not in this phase.** Review pages, casino card invention, or new editorial structures.

### Phase 6 — Public-Readiness Sweep

- **Goal.** Apply all Public-Readiness Gates across migrated surfaces before any broader public exposure.
- **Entry.** All prior phases are complete for the surfaces being swept.
- **Exit.** Every migrated page passes all nine gates per page; failing pages are gated from navigation and public index.
- **Not in this phase.** New page creation, new components, content expansion, or redefinition of gate criteria.

### Phase 7 — Visual Bible preparation

- **Goal.** Consolidate the constitutionally conforming surface set as the input substrate for the HybridBonus Visual Bible.
- **Entry.** All Public-Readiness Gates are passed for the surfaces that will feed the Visual Bible.
- **Exit.** The conforming surface set is documented, stable, and ready as the Visual Bible substrate; E3 has not authored the Visual Bible but has prepared the conditions for it.
- **Not in this phase.** Visual Bible authorship, new visual language, component redefinition, or additional editorial content.

## 5. Prototype Migration Rules

Every prototype element receives an explicit disposition: **Remove / Neutralize / Defer / Replace / Migrate**. No element is silently deleted from constitutional memory — every disposition is logged in the operational status register.

- **Placeholder casino cards.** Neutralize in Phase 1 (strip commercial affordances, ranking, and decision cues). Replace or migrate only once E2 supplies the conforming card components and Phase 3 trust surfaces exist.
- **Example casino names.** Neutralize as illustrative-only in Phase 1; remove or replace with verified entities only when real, sourced data is available.
- **Topplista / ranking language.** Remove on contact. Ranking, "topplista", "bäst i test", and total-score framing are constitutionally forbidden and have no migration path.
- **Outbound casino CTAs beside incomplete reviews.** Neutralize in Phase 1 (no live commercial outbound from non-conforming review surfaces). Reintroduce only after the review passes Phase 4 exit and Phase 6 gates.
- **Unfinished FAQ sections.** Neutralize or defer in Phase 1; reintroduce only when content conforms to layer rules and sourcing.
- **Trust modules not built from approved Trust-family components.** Neutralize in Phase 1; replace with E2 Trust-family components in Phase 3. No bespoke trust visualization.
- **Responsible-gambling visibility.** Must be present before any commercial action on any surface from Phase 3 onward. If absent, the surface is gated from public exposure.
- **Navigation items leading to thin or unfinished pages.** Neutralize (remove from public navigation) in Phase 1; reintroduce only when the destination passes Phase 6 gates.

## 6. Public-Readiness Gates (per page)

1. No placeholder content.
2. No live commercial CTAs adjacent to incomplete reviews.
3. No ranking or total-score language.
4. Responsible gambling visible before any commercial action.
5. Trust / Disclosure components present before decision points.
6. Mobile readability verified (no horizontal scroll; internal table scroll).
7. No unapproved patterns or components outside E2.
8. No Layer 3 recommendation outside slutsats; no Layer 1 factual claim without a source slot.
9. Page-level publication metadata readiness verified where required by OS / Engineering. **E3 verifies readiness only; it does not define SEO strategy or implementation — that remains owned by the Operating System, Engineering governance, or an approved SEO standard where one exists.**

**Gate enforcement:** Gates are pass/fail per page, not site-wide averages. A single failing gate blocks the page, not the whole site — but the site index and navigation must not point at blocked pages.

## 7. Work Mode Rules

- **Build Mode (safe):** applying approved E2 components, Phase 1 neutralization, copy fixes within editorial rules, SEO metadata under existing standards, favicon/asset swaps, bug fixes, and routine maintenance that does not affect editorial meaning, components, governance, IA, or public-readiness gates.
- **Plan Mode (required):** new sections, new E2 components or families, component-contract changes, phase or gate changes, multi-phase migrations, reintroducing any Phase 1 neutralized element, IA changes, public-readiness decisions.
- Wait for E2 contracts before building surfaces that depend on them.
- Deeper audit required when a change touches multiple constitutional layers.
- **Silence is not permission** — strict for editorial implementation work, phase/gate changes, component migration, IA, and public-readiness decisions. Routine maintenance may proceed under Build Mode only when it does not affect editorial meaning, components, governance, IA, or public-readiness gates.

## 8. Dependency Rules

1. E3 consumes E0/E1/E2; it never redefines them.
2. If a needed component, principle, or contract is missing, work pauses and escalates to the owning constitution rather than inventing locally.
3. No phase begins for a surface until the prior phase's exit criteria are met for that surface.
4. Trust-bearing requirements from E0 and E1, including E2 Trust-family components where applicable, must be constitutionally supported before commercial or decision-driving surfaces are activated.
5. Cross-constitutional change (a need that spans OS, E0, E1, or E2) is escalated under the governance priority (OS > Engineering > Editorial > Design) before E3 proceeds.

---

## 9. Anti-Patterns

1. Inventing components outside E2.
2. Redefining E0/E1 principles inside E3.
3. Introducing total scores, ranking, or sales tone.
4. Silently deleting prototype elements (every disposition must be logged).
5. Bypassing public-readiness gates for speed.
6. Mixing layers within a sentence (Layer 1 / Layer 2 / Layer 3).
7. Treating E3 as a visual or copy specification.
8. Reintroducing a Phase 1 neutralized element without Plan Mode and explicit gate clearance.

## 10. Amendment Rules

1. Constitutional changes to E3 require formal amendment.
2. Operational status tracking is not constitutional and may be updated without amendment.
3. Amendments must cite the OS/E0/E1/E2 sections they depend on; E3 may not amend upstream constitutions.
4. An amendment that would alter the phase model, gates, dependency rules, or anti-patterns requires Plan Mode and explicit approval.
5. Amendments are recorded in E3 itself; operational consequences are recorded in the separate operational status register.

## 11. Status Tracking

E3 maintains a **separate operational implementation status register**, distinct from the constitutional text, at `mem://editorial/e3-status`. Operational — updatable without amendment.

The register records phase progress, per-surface dispositions from the Prototype Migration Rules, and gate sign-offs. It never contains constitutional rules.
