## 16. BonusTermsTable

- **Purpose**: Present the core factual terms of a casino bonus in a structured, scannable format so the reader can quickly understand the practical conditions attached to the bonus before reading deeper editorial explanation.
- **Editorial meaning**: Layer 1 clarity surface for bonus facts. An editorial transparency component, not a ranking component, offer card, conversion widget, or promotional banner. Confirmed terms are visibly distinguished from editorial explanation.
- **Reader problem solved**: Answers — What is the bonus? Who can use it? What wagering or turnover requirement applies? What deposit, time, game, payment, or withdrawal limitations matter? What source and freshness support the information?
- **Usage conditions**: Allowed on casino review pages, bonus explainer pages, bonus terms education pages, licensed-operator comparison pages used neutrally, and methodology-supported editorial pages. Swedish-licensed operator contexts only. Supports the following fields where available: bonus type, bonus value, minimum deposit, maximum bonus amount, wagering requirement, eligible games, game contribution notes, time limit, payment method restrictions, withdrawal restrictions, new/existing customer eligibility, important exclusions, source date, last checked date, responsible gambling reminder or link adjacency. Fields may be omitted only when information is genuinely unavailable or not applicable; unknown values must not be guessed — render "Verifiering tillkommer". Layout: readability over density; clear two-column or grouped-label structure on mobile; no horizontal overflow; no cramped desktop multi-column table forced onto mobile; long terms wrap cleanly; field labels understandable without legal knowledge; important restrictive terms remain visible, never hidden behind decorative UI or collapsed by default; source/update status visible near the table. Visitor-facing copy in plain-language Swedish — neutral, clear, cautious, non-promotional, free from urgency and exaggerated value claims. Bonus terms are treated as time-sensitive: support last checked date, source label, manual editorial verification status, and an optional warning when terms may have changed. Never implies live accuracy unless the underlying data source supports it.
- **Required relationships**:
  - Source discipline: each material fact aligns with SourceReference / VerificationStatus; the page-level SourcesRegister must resolve every cited source.
  - Freshness: a Stale state requires an adjacent DataStatusNotice (per Batch 1 thresholds).
  - Responsible gambling adjacency (approved rule C): on any page containing BonusTermsTable tied to a real operator bonus that also carries a commercial CTA, the page's ResponsibleGamblingBlock must already precede the first CTA (per Batch 1); BonusTermsTable does not satisfy that requirement itself. On pure educational pages with no CTA, a compact responsible-gambling reference must be present in the same section.
  - RiskNotice interaction (approved rule D): a high wagering multiplier, restrictive max-bet, short time limit, or similarly serious restriction surfaced inside BonusTermsTable counts toward the containing section's single-RiskNotice budget per Batch 1 (no stacking). Serious restrictions must remain clearly visible either inside the component itself or compounded into the single allowed RiskNotice for that section — never silently dropped to respect the budget.
  - May compose with WageringBreakdown, ReviewSection, ArticleLead, OperatorIdentity, InfoBlock, and review methodology components. Must not replace deeper editorial analysis.
- **Supported states** (approved decision A):
  - Complete — all material terms verified and within the freshness window.
  - Partial — some fields genuinely unavailable; rendered as "Verifiering tillkommer", never guessed.
  - Stale — past freshness threshold; adjacent DataStatusNotice required.
  - Pending — no verified terms yet; the table does not render; a placeholder notice renders instead.
- **Forbidden uses**: Countdown timers; flashing offer states; jackpot visuals; "limited time" pressure styling; winner ribbons; ranking numbers; total-score badges; "best casino" / "top" / "highest value" / "winner" framing; dark-pattern CTA hierarchy; hiding or collapsing critical restrictions by default; claim-pressure language; implied live accuracy without a supporting source; use outside Swedish-licensed operator contexts; substitution for SourcesRegister, ResponsibleGamblingBlock, or deeper editorial analysis.
- **Related components**: WageringBreakdown, ReviewSection, ArticleLead, OperatorIdentity, InfoBlock, SourceReference, SourcesRegister, VerificationStatus, DataStatusNotice, RiskNotice, ResponsibleGamblingBlock, AffiliateDisclosure, EditorialIndependenceNotice.

## 17. WageringBreakdown

- **Purpose**: Explain how a wagering requirement works in practical terms, translating a bonus condition into a plain-language example without making gambling feel easier, safer, or more profitable than it is.
- **Editorial meaning**: Educational Layer 2 surface anchored to a verified Layer 1 wagering fact (or clearly labeled as a generic example). Not a promotional calculator, gambling-encouragement tool, conversion mechanic, or strategy guide.
- **Reader problem solved**: Answers — What does "omsättningskrav" mean? How much must be wagered in practice? Which amount is multiplied? Do all games count equally? What restrictions can make the requirement harder? Why should the visitor read the full terms before acting?
- **Usage conditions**: Allowed on bonus terms pages, casino review pages, educational guides, bonus comparison pages used neutrally, and responsible-gambling-adjacent explanatory sections. Supports: wagering multiplier; base amount used for calculation; example calculation; plain-language explanation; game contribution warning; time-limit warning; maximum bet warning where relevant; withdrawal limitation warning where relevant; reminder to read full terms; responsible gambling adjacency. Example calculation rule: examples are educational and neutral — allowed form, "Om bonusen är 100 kr och omsättningskravet är 35x behöver 3 500 kr omsättas innan bonusvillkoret är uppfyllt." Forbidden forms include "Så här låser du enklast upp bonusen." Layout: requirement summary → calculation example → practical restrictions → responsible gambling / full-terms reminder; vertically readable on mobile with no horizontal overflow; calculation is visually clear but never styled like a prize, win, jackpot, or reward meter. Visitor copy is Swedish, plain-language, careful — explains and warns; avoids excitement, tactical gambling advice, implied guarantees, and any suggestion that completion is easy. Where the component explains a real operator bonus it inherits operator source discipline (last checked date, source reference, operator-specific caveat, "terms may change" note where appropriate). Generic educational examples must be clearly presented as examples, not as live operator terms.
- **Required relationships**:
  - Source discipline: operator-bound usage aligns with SourceReference / VerificationStatus and resolves through the page's SourcesRegister; generic educational usage is explicitly labeled as an example.
  - Freshness: a Stale operator-bound state requires an adjacent DataStatusNotice.
  - Responsible gambling adjacency (approved rule C): on any page with WageringBreakdown tied to a real operator bonus that also carries a commercial CTA, the page's ResponsibleGamblingBlock must already precede the first CTA; WageringBreakdown does not satisfy that requirement itself. On pure educational pages with no CTA, a compact responsible-gambling reference must be present in the same section.
  - RiskNotice interaction (approved rule D): risks surfaced via the calculation (high multiplier, restrictive max-bet, short time limit, etc.) count toward the containing section's single-RiskNotice budget per Batch 1 (no stacking). Serious restrictions must remain clearly visible either inside the component itself or compounded into the single allowed RiskNotice for that section.
  - May compose with BonusTermsTable, ReviewSection, ArticleLead, InfoBlock, review methodology sections, and bonus explainer pages. Must not become a standalone promotional offer component.
- **Supported states** (approved decision B):
  - Generic educational — no operator binding; clearly labeled as an example.
  - Operator-bound — tied to a specific verified bonus; inherits BonusTermsTable freshness.
  - Stale operator-bound — past freshness threshold; adjacent DataStatusNotice required.
- **Forbidden uses**: "Unlock faster" / "beat the wagering" framing; strategy recommendations; expected-value claims; bonus-maximization framing; "best value" framing; winner or ranking language; urgency, FOMO, or countdown styling; bonus-progress gamification; jackpot or slot-machine visuals; aggressive CTA placement; ranking or scoring mechanics; presenting wagering as a strategy, loophole, or optimization path; implying guaranteed value or easy completion; presenting a generic example as live operator terms.
- **Related components**: BonusTermsTable, ReviewSection, ArticleLead, InfoBlock, SourceReference, SourcesRegister, VerificationStatus, DataStatusNotice, RiskNotice, ResponsibleGamblingBlock, AffiliateDisclosure, EditorialIndependenceNotice.
