## Batch 4 — Decision Support

### StrengthsAndLimitations

**Purpose.** Present a balanced, side-by-side synthesis of an operator's or
product's editorial strengths and limitations as Layer 2 analysis derived
from Layer 1 verified facts.

**Editorial meaning.** Signals that HybridBonus has weighed both sides and
refuses to hide weaknesses. Embodies the trust-before-conversion rule:
strengths and limitations carry equal visual and structural weight.

**Reader problem solved.** Readers comparing options need an honest, scannable
view of trade-offs without sifting through long prose. Removes the cognitive
load of inferring weaknesses from marketing-style bullet lists elsewhere on
the web.

**Usage conditions.** Used inside ReviewSection blocks where a balanced
synthesis is editorially warranted (typically after factual sections and
before EditorialVerdict). Requires at least one entry in each side; never
rendered with strengths only or limitations only.

**Required relationships.** Sits downstream of verified Layer 1 sections;
upstream of EditorialVerdict. Must coexist with SourceReference for any
claim that introduces a fact not already cited in the section. Must respect
responsible-gambling adjacency rules where applicable (RiskNotice precedes
first commercial CTA, not this component).

**Supported states.**
- Balanced (both sides populated, parity in weight and tone).
- Provisional (review still under verification — limitations side must
  carry a DataStatusNotice or VerificationStatus indicator; strengths must
  not appear without matching disclosure).

**Forbidden uses.** Never styled as a marketing pros/cons bullet block.
Limitations side never visually de-emphasized (no smaller type, muted
color treatment beyond semantic parity, collapsed-by-default, or
secondary placement). Never used to manufacture artificial balance by
inventing limitations or downplaying strengths. Never used as a substitute
for EditorialVerdict.

**Related components.** ReviewSection, EditorialVerdict, SourceReference,
DataStatusNotice, VerificationStatus, RiskNotice.

### BestFitGuidance

**Purpose.** Orient readers toward whether an operator or product fits their
player profile, in profile terms — never as a ranking or recommendation.

**Editorial meaning.** Encodes the Editorial Constitution's player-profile
philosophy: the same operator can be a good fit for one player and a poor
fit for another. Replaces the impulse to declare a universal "best".

**Reader problem solved.** Readers asking "is this for me?" need a direct,
honest answer rooted in their preferences (deposit size, payout speed,
game preferences, bonus tolerance, risk posture) rather than a generic
score.

**Usage conditions.** Used on reviews and comparison-adjacent guides after
strengths/limitations have been established. Requires both a "fits players
who…" section and a "fits less well for players who…" section; never
rendered with only one side.

**Required relationships.** Downstream of StrengthsAndLimitations; upstream
of or adjacent to EditorialVerdict. Must respect responsible-gambling
adjacency rules; never frames high-risk behavior as a "fit".

**Supported states.**
- Standard (both fit and non-fit populated).
- Provisional (review under verification — carries DataStatusNotice; both
  sides still required).

**Forbidden uses.** Never used as a ranking proxy ("best for high rollers"
as a leaderboard signal). Never recommends a player who shows risk
indicators (chasing losses, bonus hunting framed positively, etc.).
Never substitutes for EditorialVerdict. Never frames a non-fit as a
weakness of the reader.

**Related components.** StrengthsAndLimitations, EditorialVerdict,
ResponsibleGamblingBlock, RiskNotice, DataStatusNotice.

### RelatedReading

**Purpose.** Surface a curated set of internal editorial links that
materially extend the reader's understanding of the current page's topic.

**Editorial meaning.** Embodies the internal-linking mandate: no page is a
dead end; every page connects to relevant editorial context. Reinforces
that HybridBonus is a connected body of work, not a directory of isolated
pages.

**Reader problem solved.** Helps the reader take the next informed step
without re-searching — comparison from a review, methodology from a
verdict, responsible-gambling context from a bonus explainer.

**Usage conditions.** Rendered near the end of long-form pages (reviews,
guides, comparison pages). Hard cap of 6 items. All links must be
internal and editorially curated; selection is either human curation or
deterministic editorial metadata (e.g. same operator family, same review
framework section, same guide series).

**Required relationships.** Sibling of SourcesList (which lists external
sources). Coexists with Breadcrumbs as orientation; the two serve
different orientation needs and do not substitute for each other.

**Supported states.**
- Curated (1–6 human- or metadata-selected internal links).
- Empty / hidden (no editorially valid related items — component is not
  rendered; never padded with weak matches).

**Forbidden uses.** Never selected by engagement signals (popular,
trending, click-through-derived, conversion-derived, dwell-time-derived).
Never uses clickbait labels. Never links to external sites. Never used
to inflate session depth. Never exceeds 6 items, even in "see all"
treatments.

**Related components.** Breadcrumbs, SourcesList, ReviewSection,
TableOfContents.

## Batch 5 — Comparison & Navigation

### ComparisonTable

**Purpose.** Present a structured side-by-side trade-off analysis of a small
set of operators or products against a fixed, editorially defined,
TrustProfile-compatible row schema.

**Editorial meaning.** Encodes the comparison philosophy: comparisons exist
to expose trade-offs, never to crown a winner. The fixed row schema
guarantees parity; no operator gets a custom advantage row.

**Reader problem solved.** Readers choosing between a small number of
options need a parity-enforced view of how each option performs on the
same dimensions, without algorithmic ranking obscuring the trade-offs.

**Usage conditions.** Used on jämför-pages and inside guides that
legitimately compare a small operator set. Hard cap of 6 operators per
table. Row schema is fixed editorially and TrustProfile-compatible; the
canonical row set is not locked at the E2.1 layer and is governed by
engineering/editorial memory downstream. Table scrolls internally on
narrow screens; the page itself never gains a horizontal scrollbar.

**Required relationships.** Each operator cell that surfaces a TrustProfile
category must reflect the same canonical TrustProfile values used in that
operator's review (no per-comparison re-scoring). Must coexist with
AffiliateDisclosure and ResponsibleGamblingBlock on commercial pages.
Any operator-bound bonus information must respect BonusTermsTable and
WageringBreakdown contract semantics (no inline restating that diverges
from the operator's review).

**Supported states.**
- Standard (2–6 operators; full row parity).
- Provisional (one or more operators carry DataStatusNotice or
  VerificationStatus — the indicator surfaces in-row; the table is not
  hidden but the affected cells must not be presented as verified).

**Forbidden uses.** No leaderboards. No total scores or aggregate numeric
ranking. No "winner" column, badge, highlight row, or visual emphasis
that functions as a ranking proxy. No "best value" / "editor's pick"
framing inside the table. No engagement- or conversion-derived ordering.
Any qualitative summary row must describe trade-offs only and must not
function as an aggregate verdict, winner row, ranking proxy, or
replacement for EditorialVerdict. Never exceeds 6 operators.

**Related components.** TrustProfile, EditorialVerdict, BonusTermsTable,
WageringBreakdown, AffiliateDisclosure, ResponsibleGamblingBlock,
RiskNotice, DataStatusNotice, VerificationStatus.

### Breadcrumbs

**Purpose.** Provide path-based orientation on every undersida, reflecting
the canonical URL structure.

**Editorial meaning.** Reinforces the site's information architecture as a
coherent, navigable hierarchy. Signals to the reader (and to search
engines via BreadcrumbList microdata) where the current page sits in the
editorial body of work.

**Reader problem solved.** Lets readers understand their location and
move up the hierarchy without relying on browser back navigation or
guessing at site structure.

**Usage conditions.** Rendered on every undersida; never on startsidan.
Each crumb above the current page must point to a real, navigable page
in the canonical URL structure. The "Hem" root crumb is always present.

**Required relationships.** Mirrors the canonical URL structure defined
in engineering memory. Paired with BreadcrumbList JSON-LD on every page
it renders.

**Supported states.**
- Standard (full path from Hem to current page; all parents navigable).
- Deep (longer paths scroll internally inside the component on narrow
  screens; the page never gains a horizontal scrollbar).

**Forbidden uses.** No fabricated parent pages. Synthetic-category parents
are permitted only when backed by a real navigable page; a synthetic
crumb without a destination is forbidden. Never used as primary
navigation. Never reordered to flatter the current page.

**Related components.** SiteHeader, RelatedReading, TableOfContents.
