# E2.1 — Batch 1 Component Contracts

Scope: 10 Batch 1 components persisted from the approved E2.1 registry. Parent: E2 — Editorial Component Library (mem://editorial/e2-component-library). Contracts follow E2's eight-field template. Substance is unchanged from the approved plan.

## 1. TrustProfile

- **Purpose**: Visualize HybridBonus's editorial assessment of an operator across the six fixed Trust Profile categories (Trygghet, Bonusvärde, Omsättningsvillkor, Uttag, Spelupplevelse, Helhetsvärde) as qualitative per-category judgments with short rationale.
- **Editorial meaning**: A structured, multi-dimensional editorial opinion. Canonical surface where HybridBonus's Layer 2 analysis of an operator is consolidated. Not a score, not a ranking input, not a marketing badge.
- **Reader problem solved**: Reader needs to understand *where* an operator is strong or weak before reading 17 sections, without being misled by a single number.
- **Usage conditions**: Operator review pages only. Renders once per review, near the top of the review body (after ArticleLead, before deep sections). All six categories must be present; unverified categories render the internal "Verifiering tillkommer" marker, never silently omitted.
- **Required relationships**:
  - Must coexist with EditorialVerdict on the same review page; EditorialVerdict references TrustProfile.
  - Categories are fixed and ordered per OS memory; component must not accept arbitrary category lists.
  - Each category may anchor-link to its corresponding review section but must not duplicate it.
  - Adjacent DataStatusNotice is required when (a) two or more categories are pending/stale, or (b) any *core* category (Trygghet, Bonusvärde, Omsättningsvillkor, Uttag) is pending or stale. A single non-core pending category uses only the internal marker.
- **Supported states**: Complete (all six assessed); Partial-minor (one non-core category pending — internal marker only); Partial-material (≥2 pending, or any core pending — adjacent DataStatusNotice required); Stale (assessment past freshness threshold — adjacent DataStatusNotice required; never self-styled as urgent).
- **Forbidden uses**: No total score, aggregate number, star count, percentage, grade, or letter. No algorithmic-weighting explanation. No ranking against other operators inside the component. No use on comparison, listing, or homepages. No standalone "winner" / "best" / "top pick" labels. Never a CTA surface.
- **Related components**: EditorialVerdict (paired), ReviewSection (per-category anchors), DataStatusNotice (threshold above), SourceReference, VerificationStatus.

## 2. EditorialVerdict

- **Purpose**: Express HybridBonus's qualitative recommendation framing for an operator, grounded in TrustProfile, in plain Swedish.
- **Editorial meaning**: Layer 3 recommendation surface, allowed outside slutsats only when bound to TrustProfile. Articulates *for whom* the operator fits and *for whom* it does not.
- **Reader problem solved**: A clear, honest "should I consider this?" framing without a score or hype.
- **Usage conditions**: Review pages only. Always paired with TrustProfile on the same page. Phrased as profile-fit guidance ("passar spelare som...", "passar mindre bra för..."), never as universal verdict. May render in the editorial body above the commercial CTA gate, but never inside or visually attached to the CTA region.
- **Required relationships**:
  - Must reference TrustProfile on the same page (visually adjacent or anchored).
  - On pages with a commercial CTA: ResponsibleGamblingBlock must precede the first CTA; EditorialVerdict sits in the editorial body above that gate.
  - Must not appear without an underlying review body containing the 17-section architecture.
- **Supported states**: Standard (TrustProfile complete); Conditional (TrustProfile partial — verdict explicitly notes which dimensions are unverified); Withheld (insufficient verified facts — renders explanation, not a guess).
- **Forbidden uses**: Never a free-floating badge, pill, sticker, ribbon, medal, or score chip. Never reused on comparison, listing, category, or home pages. Never phrased as "bäst", "vinnare", "topp 1". Never includes urgency or time-bound framing. Never acts as or substitutes for a CTA; "Hämta bonusen" is never a verdict. Never reduces to a single word/number/grade. Never visually attached to or embedded inside the CTA region.
- **Related components**: TrustProfile (mandatory pair), ResponsibleGamblingBlock, BestFitGuidance, StrengthsAndLimitations.

## 3. VerificationStatus

- **Purpose**: Mark a specific claim or fact with its verification state and last-verified moment.
- **Editorial meaning**: Claim-level Layer 1 integrity signal.
- **Reader problem solved**: Reader needs to know which individual facts are reliable right now, especially for volatile data (licens, bonusbelopp, uttagstider, betalmetoder).
- **Usage conditions**: Attaches to a single factual claim (inline or adjacent). Used wherever a discrete fact has a verification lifecycle. Not used for opinion, analysis, or recommendation. Must be accessible and discoverable by default — Visual Bible may choose always-visible, compact, or progressive disclosure, but never hover-only / pointer-only revelation.
- **Required relationships**:
  - Should normally accompany or link to a SourceReference for the same claim.
  - Page/section-level aggregate freshness is expressed by DataStatusNotice, not by stacking VerificationStatus chips.
- **Supported states**: Verified (with date); Pending ("Verifiering tillkommer"); Stale (past freshness threshold); Disputed (conflicting sources — neutral note, never alarmist).
- **Forbidden uses**: Not used on Layer 2 or Layer 3 sentences. Not used as a trust badge for an entire operator or page. Never decorative. Never invents dates or sources. Must not be hidden behind hover-only behavior or otherwise inaccessible by default.
- **Related components**: SourceReference, SourcesRegister, DataStatusNotice, TrustProfile.

## 4. SourceReference

- **Purpose**: Attribute a single factual claim to its underlying source(s).
- **Editorial meaning**: Atomic citation unit for Layer 1 facts.
- **Reader problem solved**: Trace a claim ("licensierat av Spelinspektionen", "omsättningskrav 35x") back to its origin.
- **Usage conditions**: Inline at the claim, or as a footnote-style marker resolving into SourcesRegister. Required for any externally verifiable Layer 1 fact in reviews and guides.
- **Required relationships**:
  - Every SourceReference on a page must resolve into SourcesRegister.
  - Must align with the VerificationStatus state of the same claim (Verified claims must have at least one resolvable SourceReference).
- **Supported states**: Primary (official/authoritative source, e.g. Spelinspektionen); Secondary (operator's own published terms); Pending ("Källa tillkommer" — never fabricated).
- **Forbidden uses**: Never cites an opinion or HybridBonus's own analysis. Never invents URLs, organisations, or document titles. Never decorative. Not used at page level — that is SourcesRegister.
- **Related components**: SourcesRegister, VerificationStatus. (MethodologyReference relationship deferred until that component is contracted.)

## 5. SourcesRegister

- **Purpose**: Provide a complete, page-level list of all sources referenced on the page in a stable, scannable format.
- **Editorial meaning**: Transparency surface for the page's evidentiary base.
- **Reader problem solved**: A single place to audit every source the page relies on.
- **Usage conditions**: Renders once per page that contains any SourceReference. Placed at a predictable late position (before the responsible-gambling footer area). Lists every distinct source cited at least once.
- **Required relationships**:
  - Must contain every source referenced by any SourceReference on the page (no orphans, no unused entries).
  - Aligns with DataStatusNotice when the page declares known data limitations.
  - No required dependency on MethodologyReference in Batch 1; relationship may be added when MethodologyReference is contracted.
- **Supported states**: Complete; Partial (some sources pending — explicitly marked); Empty (page has no factual claims — component does not render).
- **Forbidden uses**: Never used at claim level. Never padded with unrelated authoritative-looking links to inflate trust. Never used on pages with no Layer 1 claims.
- **Related components**: SourceReference, VerificationStatus, DataStatusNotice, EditorialIndependenceNotice.

## 6. EditorialIndependenceNotice

- **Purpose**: Disclose HybridBonus's editorial-independence policy and its boundary with any commercial relationships.
- **Editorial meaning**: Standing transparency statement about editorial governance. Distinct from AffiliateDisclosure (which discloses a specific commercial relationship).
- **Reader problem solved**: Reader needs to know that Trust Profile, verdicts, and analysis are not dictated by commercial deals.
- **Usage conditions**: The full statement lives on the methodology / about policy surface. Review, guide, and comparison pages render a compact reference linking to the full statement. AffiliateDisclosure remains separate and mandatory wherever commercial links exist.
- **Required relationships**:
  - Compact reference on a page must link to the full canonical statement.
  - Coexists with AffiliateDisclosure on any page containing a commercial CTA — neither replaces the other.
  - Pairs with SourcesRegister as the page's transparency cluster.
- **Supported states**: Full (canonical statement on policy surface); Compact reference (in-page, with link to full); Page-specific note (rare — only when a page has unusual conditions worth surfacing).
- **Forbidden uses**: Never implies endorsement, awards, or third-party certification HybridBonus does not hold. Never replaces AffiliateDisclosure. Never used as marketing copy. Compact form must always link to the full statement — never stands alone without resolution.
- **Related components**: AffiliateDisclosure, SourcesRegister, DataStatusNotice, ResponsibleGamblingBlock.

## 7. AffiliateDisclosure

- **Purpose**: Clearly disclose that specific outbound links on the page are commercial/affiliate links and what that means for the reader.
- **Editorial meaning**: Legal-and-ethical disclosure tied to the presence of commercial outbound links. Independent of editorial-assessment quality.
- **Reader problem solved**: Reader must know, before they click, that an outbound link is commercial and that HybridBonus may earn from it — without that disclosure being buried.
- **Usage conditions**: Required on any page with at least one affiliate/commercial outbound link. The first commercial CTA on a page must be preceded by, or immediately adjacent to, the full AffiliateDisclosure. Subsequent repeated CTAs on the same page may use a compact disclosure, provided the first full disclosure has already appeared above them in reading order.
- **Required relationships**:
  - Must coexist with EditorialIndependenceNotice (compact reference) on the same page.
  - First CTA on the page must have the full disclosure before/adjacent; later CTAs may use compact.
  - Must coexist with ResponsibleGamblingBlock, which precedes the first commercial CTA.
- **Supported states**: Full (default; required before/adjacent to first CTA); Compact (inline near repeated CTAs after the full disclosure has appeared); Expanded (dedicated section, e.g. /om-oss).
- **Forbidden uses**: Never hidden behind a tooltip or collapsed-only disclosure not visible on first paint near the first CTA. Never worded to minimise the commercial relationship. Never substituted for EditorialIndependenceNotice. Compact form never appears before the full form has appeared on the page.
- **Related components**: EditorialIndependenceNotice, ResponsibleGamblingBlock, CTA surfaces ("Besök casinot").

## 8. DataStatusNotice

- **Purpose**: Communicate page-level or section-level limitations in data completeness, freshness, or verification.
- **Editorial meaning**: Honest scope/freshness statement about a region of content as a whole.
- **Reader problem solved**: Reader can calibrate trust when a page or section is partial, in-progress, or based on data that may be stale.
- **Usage conditions**: Used at the top of an affected page or section. Mandatory adjacent to TrustProfile when (a) two or more TrustProfile categories are pending/stale, or (b) any core category (Trygghet, Bonusvärde, Omsättningsvillkor, Uttag) is pending or stale. Also mandatory when verification of major claims is pending or a comparison set is known to be incomplete. Not used for a single non-core pending TrustProfile category — the internal marker suffices.
- **Required relationships**:
  - Complements VerificationStatus (claim-level) and SourcesRegister (sources-level) by giving the aggregate picture.
  - On reviews, must render adjacent to TrustProfile when the thresholds above are met.
- **Supported states**: Partial data; Pending verification; Stale (past freshness threshold); In progress / draft scope.
- **Forbidden uses**: Never alarmist, never urgent, never styled to manufacture FOMO. Never excuses fabricated facts — partial means "missing", not "guessed". Never a decorative "updated" badge. Not used at single-claim granularity — that is VerificationStatus.
- **Related components**: VerificationStatus, SourcesRegister, TrustProfile, RiskNotice.

## 9. ResponsibleGamblingBlock

- **Purpose**: Surface responsible-gambling information and tools (Spelpaus, insättningsgränser, stödlinjen) in a consistent, prominent block.
- **Editorial meaning**: Non-negotiable duty-of-care surface. Constitutional precondition for any commercial action on the page.
- **Reader problem solved**: Reader (especially at-risk reader) must have frictionless access to self-exclusion and support resources before encountering a commercial CTA.
- **Usage conditions**: Mandatory on every page containing a commercial CTA or operator-specific bonus/promotional content. Must appear before the first commercial CTA in reading order, not only in the footer. Also appears on guide and information pages where relevant.
- **Required relationships**:
  - Must precede the first commercial CTA in reading order on any page that contains one.
  - Coexists with AffiliateDisclosure and EditorialIndependenceNotice (compact reference) on commercial pages.
  - May coexist with RiskNotice when specific risk-bearing facts are present.
- **Supported states**: Standard (full block); Compact (reinforcement near additional CTAs once full block has appeared above); Expanded (dedicated guide/landing context).
- **Forbidden uses**: Never reduced to a single legal-fine-print line. Never visually de-emphasized relative to commercial CTAs. Never marketing copy ("spela ansvarsfullt och vinn mer"). Never replaced by RiskNotice.
- **Related components**: AffiliateDisclosure, EditorialIndependenceNotice, RiskNotice, EditorialVerdict.

## 10. RiskNotice

- **Purpose**: Flag a specific risk-bearing fact or pattern (e.g. very high wagering requirement, restrictive withdrawal cap, opaque T&Cs) in context.
- **Editorial meaning**: Local, fact-anchored caution attached to one specific element of analysis. Layer 2 in tone, always anchored to a verified Layer 1 fact.
- **Reader problem solved**: Reader understands *why a particular thing matters as a risk* at the point in the page where they encounter it.
- **Usage conditions**: Used inline next to the specific fact or analysis it qualifies (e.g. inside WageringBreakdown, BonusTermsTable, ComparisonTable cell, or a review section). Specific, evidence-bound, proportionate. Default maximum is one RiskNotice per review section; multiple closely related risks must be combined into a single compound RiskNotice rather than stacked.
- **Required relationships**:
  - Must be anchored to a concrete fact backed by SourceReference / VerificationStatus.
  - Does not satisfy or replace ResponsibleGamblingBlock; the two coexist when commercial action is on the page.
- **Supported states**: Standard (single specific risk); Compound (multiple closely related risks summarised in one notice with itemised facts).
- **Forbidden uses**: Never generic fear-mongering. Never without an underlying verified fact. Never decorative. Never a substitute for ResponsibleGamblingBlock. Never urgent, alarmist, or styled like a system error. Never stacked — more than one per section requires compounding.
- **Related components**: ResponsibleGamblingBlock, VerificationStatus, SourceReference, WageringBreakdown, BonusTermsTable, ComparisonTable.
